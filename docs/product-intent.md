# Follow Through Product Intent

This is not a generic task manager.

Follow Through exists to solve one problem:

**Follow-through in real life, especially inside a relationship.**

The app is not about tracking tasks, productivity, or organization for its own sake.

The app is about:

- Doing what I said I would do
- Staying aligned with my partner
- Making sure important things do not silently fall through the cracks

## What Makes This App Different

Tasks represent commitments, not to-dos.

A task should answer:

- What did I say I would do?
- What does done actually look like?
- Why does this matter in real life?
- Who is affected if this slips?

## Core Product Beliefs

### Tasks are commitments

A task is not just an item in a list. It is something someone is relying on, expecting, or carrying mentally.

### Done when defines real-world completion

Every important task should have a clear finish line. "Done" should mean the real-world issue is resolved, not just that a checkbox was tapped.

### Why this matters reflects real-life impact

The why should not sound like productivity language. It should explain the human impact:

- reducing stress
- avoiding confusion
- preventing resentment
- keeping trust
- making life easier for the other person

### Weekly check-ins keep alignment alive

The weekly check-in exists to make sure we actually talk about what is happening, what is slipping, and what needs to change.

### Date nights protect the relationship

Date nights are not an extra feature. They exist because follow-through includes making time for the relationship itself.

### Notifications create awareness, not pressure

Notifications should not guilt, nag, or spam. They should surface what needs attention before it quietly becomes friction.

## Behavior Rules for Product Decisions

Do not build this like a normal productivity app.

Avoid:

- generic task management patterns
- corporate language
- system language
- unnecessary dashboards
- feature complexity that creates more work

Prefer:

- clarity
- follow-through
- relationship alignment
- simple decisions
- grounded language
- fewer but more meaningful prompts

## Success Criteria

The app is working if:

- I always know what actually needs to get done
- Nothing important quietly slips
- My partner does not feel like she is carrying everything
- We consistently check in and stay aligned
- Date nights stay intentional
- Repeatedly snoozed tasks turn into clearer next steps instead of guilt

## Feature Decision Filter

Before adding a feature, ask:

1. Does this make a commitment clearer?
2. Does this help something get finished in real life?
3. Does this reduce mental load for one or both partners?
4. Does this help us talk about what is slipping?
5. Does this prevent resentment or silent drift?

If the answer is no, do not build it yet.

## AI Usage Rule

AI should enhance wording and clarity. It should not control the core logic.

Good uses of AI:

- improving Done when
- improving Why this matters
- suggesting smaller next steps
- helping summarize check-in topics

Bad uses of AI:

- deciding priority by itself
- replacing deterministic notification logic
- generating vague productivity advice
- creating more noise than clarity

## Product North Star

Follow Through should make the next right commitment obvious, make completion concrete, and keep the relationship from carrying invisible unfinished work.
